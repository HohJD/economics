Economics made visual

Learn how demand and supply shape prices

This lesson focuses on one big idea: price changes cause movement along a curve, while outside forces cause the whole curve to shift. Use the graph, test the scenarios, and then try the quiz.

Core idea

Demand and supply are relationships between price and quantity. A change in the good’s own price moves you along the curve. A change in anything else shifts the curve.

Own price → movement Other factors → shift Equilibrium = balance

What to watch for

When the curve shifts, the new price is the result of a shortage or surplus. This is how economists explain market changes without mixing up cause and effect.

The big picture

Demand

Shows how much buyers want at each price. Higher prices usually reduce quantity demanded.

Supply

Shows how much sellers offer at each price. Higher prices usually increase quantity supplied.

Equilibrium

The point where buyers and sellers agree. It is where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied.

Why it matters

Markets do not only react to price changes. They also react to weather, income, tastes, technology, and policy.

Scenario explorer

Hot weather

People want more ice cream at every price. Demand shifts right.

Cold snap

People want less ice cream at every price. Demand shifts left.

Milk becomes expensive

Production costs rise, so sellers offer less at every price. Supply shifts left.

New machine lowers costs

Firms can produce more at every price. Supply shifts right.
